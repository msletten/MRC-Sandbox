Poodle Vulnerability

Last Updated: Oct 31, 2014 10:01AM PDT
On October 14th, Google discovered a vulnerability in SSLv3.0. This vulnerability allows attackers to trigger a connection failure and force your browser to use SSLv3.0, which they can then attempt to exploit. The simple workaround right now is to disable the SSLv3.0 in your browser or to use one that supports TLS_FALLBACK_SCSV, Google Chrome has used since February.

While Firefox supports SSLv3.0, you can switch it off until your servers incorporate the proper fallback fix.

Both Google and Firefox plan to disable SSLv3.0 support in upcoming releases. 

Although Mosaic is not compatible with any of the following, Internet Explorer 6 and Windows XP without SP3 should not be used due to this vulnerability.

For more information on this, you may want to read some of the following articles:
  • Stack Exchange
  • Symantec
  • Mozilla Security Blog
